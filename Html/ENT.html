Department of Ear, Nose & Throat

Infections and diseases pertaining to ear, nose and throat are so common. Almost every individual suffers from acute to severe ENT issues once in a lifetime. ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) is a branch of medicine and surgery specializing in diagnosing and treating disorders affecting the head and neck. While in earlier days, people aren’t acquainted with the concept of consulting the specialists in this field due to a lack of awareness which at times led to serious complications. Over the years, the field has evolved offering a range of wide of instruments for accurate evaluation and advanced treatment options. With the advent of better understanding and latest technologies, patients can access effective cures for sinus to tonsillitis and other associated conditions.

    • Frequently Asked Questions

    ENT specialists are trained in the medical and surgical management and treatment of patients with diseases and disorders of the ear, nose, throat (ENT), and related structures of the head and neck.

    Your ENT specialist treats several conditions from acute ear pain to hearing loss. Some of the common conditions include Tinnitus, vertigo, nasal blockage, allergies, throat pain, loss of smell or hearing, speech or voice disorders, head and neck trauma, lump in the head or neck, blood in the sputum, dysphagia, breathing issues and others.

    Depending on the reason for visiting the ENT specialist, you will undergo physical and visual examination including the areas of the ears, nose, and throat. Sometimes, the examination can make you feel overwhelmed due to the instruments used to peek, poke and prod the areas but the specialist knows the ways to comfort their patients during a physical exam. If required, you may take imaging tests for better diagnosis or may perform balance tests while experiencing any issues in your ears or signs like dizziness, lightheadedness, etc.
